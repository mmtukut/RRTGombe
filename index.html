Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of the Gombe State Revenue Recovery Tribunal?

The tribunal is established to adjudicate disputes related to revenue collection, including tax and levies, and to ensure compliance with state tax laws. It provides a fair and transparent process for resolving conflicts and enforcing revenue obligations.

How can I file a case with the tribunal?

Cases can be filed online through our case management system. You need to register on our website, provide relevant documents, and fill out the necessary forms. For detailed instructions, please visit our Filing Guidelines page.

What types of disputes does the tribunal handle?

The tribunal handles disputes related to tax assessments, tax compliance issues, penalties, and interest on unpaid taxes. We also address cases involving incorrect or unfair tax demands and provide guidance on tax laws and regulations.

Is there a fee to file a case?

Yes, there is a nominal filing fee for submitting a case to the tribunal. The fee structure varies based on the nature and complexity of the case. For a detailed breakdown, please refer to our Fees and Charges page.

How long does it take to resolve a case?

The duration of a case depends on its complexity and the availability of all required documents and information. However, we strive to resolve cases efficiently, typically within 3 to 6 months from the date of filing.

Can I appeal a decision made by the tribunal?

Yes, parties have the right to appeal decisions made by the tribunal. Appeals must be filed within 30 days of the tribunal's decision. For more details on the appeal process, please visit our Appeals Process page.
